About me

Hello everyone,
     I’m Pradeep Velmurugan from Karur, currently pursuing my Bachelor's degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering at M. Kumarasamy College of Engineering. I’ve been passionate about applying my knowledge through practical projects. One of my key projects is a Terahertz Beam Steering Antenna using Metamaterials, where I led the team and was responsible for the antenna and metamaterial design.
     Another project I worked on is Hazardous Gas Detection using RF Communication, which is designed to ensure the safety of sewage workers. I implemented the RF communication between the transmitter and receiver kits. This project has received a design patent.
     Apart from that, I have two more design patents—one for a Tomato Preservation Kit using a cooling method, and another for a Liver and Kidney Preservation Kit for medical applications.
     I’ve published two research articles: one on a THz Complementary Ring Resonator for 6G Communication in Springer Journal, and another on a Massive MIMO system using Metamaterials on ResearchGate.
     On the technical side, I’m proficient in Java and C, and have built small projects such as a Banking System using Encapsulation and an Object Detection system using basic ML techniques. I’ve also acted as a Technical Resource Person for two workshops conducted by my department and served as the Overall Coordinator for the Antenna Design Contest 2024.
     I completed a one-month internship at NIT Trichy’s R&D department, sponsored by the Government of India. Additionally, my team won the Best SolidWorks Project award at Srishti 2023.
     Outside academics, I enjoy photo and video editing, and playing cricket with friends.
